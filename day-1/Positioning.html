Positioning

Tags have the inherit position of static. These can be changed to: relative, fixed, absolute and sticky. Using directional properties with these positions we can place certain content. Ex. a fixed position to the top of the page will make an element stick to the top of the screen regardless of scrolling.

  • Static: nothing special, just flows naturally with the other content normally.
  • Relative: Setting the directional properties of the element will adjust it relative to its natural position.
  • Fixed: will be positioned relative to the display(viewport), so that even if you scroll, the page, the element will not move.
  • Absolute: will be positioned relative to the the nearest positioned ancestor(like fixed but will scroll with page).
  • Sticky: toggels between relative and fixed depending on the scroll position.
